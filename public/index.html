Dearest Lishna,

— With quiet honesty

What I Notice...

I notice the way you carry kindness like it weighs nothing.

The way your attention, when it lands on someone, makes them feel truly seen.

I notice the small things — the way you pause before answering, as if every word matters. Because to you, it does.

I notice how the room shifts, just a little, whenever you're in it.

And I notice that I've started noticing all of this — which tells me something important.

What I Feel...

It isn't fireworks, the way stories describe.

It's quieter than that. It's more like warmth — the kind that stays.

When you're around, something in me settles. And when you're not, I find myself hoping you're doing well, wherever you are.

I feel grateful — simply to know you exist in this world, and that somehow, our paths have crossed.

That gratefulness has slowly grown into something I can no longer keep to myself.

What I'm Not Asking...

I am not asking you to feel what I feel.

I am not asking you to change anything about your life, your plans, or your heart.

Feelings, when honest, do not demand — they simply arrive and offer themselves quietly.

So this is not a demand. It is not a plea. It is just a truth, offered gently, with both hands open.

Whatever you hold or set down — I will understand.

What I Do Know...

I know that some things are worth saying, even if the answer is uncertain.

I know that kindness costs nothing and that honesty, offered with care, is a form of respect.

I know that whatever happens next, I will always wish you happiness — full, beautiful, uninterrupted happiness.

And I know that writing these words, even into the uncertainty, was worth every moment.

Thank you for reading this far, Lishna.

With genuine care,
Situ 🌸
